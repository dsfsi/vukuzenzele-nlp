From domestic worker to doctor

Nonkululeko Mathebula

Former domestic  worker, Pamela  Phumzile Sithole is  proof that dreams do come true.
Born and raised in Phoe -nix in KwaZulu-Natal, the 24-year-old was able to juggle her studies with part-time work over the years having the ability to rise above her circumstances.
Despite working as a do mestic helper from the age of 14, Sithole’s perseverance and determination helped turn her life around and she recently graduated with a Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery degree from the University of Kwa Zulu-Natal. She is currently doing her internship at the Rahima Moosa Mother and Child Hospital in Johannesburg.
