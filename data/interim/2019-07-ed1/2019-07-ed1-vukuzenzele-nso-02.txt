Vuk’uzenzeleBaentšenere ba tša go aga ba baswa ba kgora tsela ya bokamoso bjo bo kgahlišagoSilusapho Nyanda Baswa ba babedi ba hlolela baswa ba  bangwe mešomo,  gomme se se kgonagetše ka tšhelete yeo ba e adimile go go tšwa go Mokgatlo wa  Tlhabollo ya Diintasteri (IDC).  Beng ba Maloka Machaba Surfacing, Kate Machaba le Matshela Maloka, ba  hirile  batho ba 28, bontšhi bja bona ke baswa, kgwebong ya bona ya sekontiri yeo e lego kua Polokwane.
Bobedi baentšenere ba tša go aga ba ba na le mengwaga ye 32, gomme ba na lerato la go ba baotledi ba ekonomi.
Kgwebo ya bona e aba ditirelo tše kgolo tše tharo: ya go apea sekontiri, go aga mabato a ditsela le tirelo ya dikoloi tše di rwalago seko ntiri sa go fiša le sa go tonya.
Sekontiri sa go fiša gantši se šomišwa go ageng ditsela tša sekontiri mola seko ntiri sa go tonya gantši se šomišwa go tswalela mekoti ditseleng. Bobedi sekontiri sa go go fiša le sa go tonya di tswakwa planteng ya khamphani gomme di tla le lengwalo la kgonthišo go tšwa go laporatori ya diteko ya entšenere ya tša kago ya Mocha Labs.
Maloka, yo e lego mo laodipharephare wa Maloka Machaba Surfacing, o rile kadimo ya tšhelete go tšwa go IDC e ba thušitše go reka metšhene ya go ikgetha ye e hlokegago go ka šoma lefapheng la sekontiri, go akaretša dilori, metšhene ya go batela ditsejana tša ka thoko ga tsela, metšhene ya go batela ditsela le metšhene ya go phagamiša dilo.
Go dira kgopelo ya kadi mo ya tšhelete go IDC Kgopelo ya kadimo ya tšhe lete ya khamphani e tsamaile ditshepetšong tše mmalwa mokgatlong wa go thuša di kgwebo ka ditšhelete. Macha ba, yo o lego molaodiphare phare wa tša ditshepedišo, o rile ba ile ba swanela ke go neela ka polane ya kgwebo ye e nyakišišitšwego gabotse ge ba be ba dira dikgopelo tša kadimo ya tšhelete.
“Netefatša gore o dirile di nyakišišo tša gago ka botlalo tša intasteri ye o nyakago go tsena go yona,” a realo, a oketša ka gore bagwebi ba swanetše ba gopole gore bjalo ka kadimo ya tšhelete, tšhe lete e swanetše go patelwa.
Maloka Machaba Surfacing e tseleng ya katlego ka lebaka la thušo ye ba e amogetšego go tšwa IDC.
Dikgopelo tša thušo ya tša ditšhelete di ka dirwa wepesaeteng goba diofising tša IDC. Mokgatlo o eletša badiradikgopelo go netefatša gore kgopelo ya bona e na le ditokumente tša maleba. Ge IDC e amogela kgopelo, e tla leboga kamogelo gomme e ka kgopela modiradikgope -lo go ka aba tshedimošo ya tlaleletšo goba ditokumente tše dingwe.
Ka morago IDC e tla neela karabelo ka go ngwala le ngwalo mabapi le  poelo ya go dira dikgopelo. Ge IDC e kgotsofetše ka maikemešetšo le ditshwanelego tše di nya kegago, modiradikgopelo o tla kgopelwa go saena ko ntraka ya kadimo ya tšhelete.

POLELO YA MAEMO A SETŠHABASouth Africa’s investment drive intensifiesAs part of building “a South Africa we want”, President Cyril Ramaphosa says government will intensify its already successful investment drive.
This will build on the R300 billion worth of investments announced during the 2018 inaugural Investment Conference. Of these, just over R250 billion worth of projects have entered implementation phase.
“We continue to build a pipeline of investments, which will be showcased at the second South African Investment Conference to be held on 5 to 7 November,” said the President.
The President made the announcement at the State of the Nation Address (SoNA) held recently in Parliament.
President Ramaphosa had appointed investment envoys to search for investors with deep pockets last year.
The President’s Special Envoys on Investment include finance  heavyweights such as former Finance Minister Trevor Manuel, former Finance Deputy Minister Mcebisi Jonas, the executive chairperson of Afropulse Group, Phumzile Langeni and chairman of Liberty Group and former CEO of Standard Bank Jacko Maree.“At a time of uncertainty, the work of the investment envoys has built important bridges between government and the business community.
From their feedback, it is clear that much more still needs to be done to improve the investment climate,” said the President.
This, he said, includes reviewing the way  government coordinates work to resolve challenges faced by investors and reforming investment promotion policy and architecture.
The private sector has committed to invest R840 billion in 43 projects over 19 sectors and to creating 155 000 jobs in the next five years.
