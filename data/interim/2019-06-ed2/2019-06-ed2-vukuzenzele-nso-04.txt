Vuk’uzenzeleMore Matshediso Le ge tlhokego ya  mešomo go bafsa e tšwela pele go ba tlhobaboroko ye e ganelelago ka Afrika Borwa, Setlamo sa Bosetšhaba sa Thušo ya Ditšhelete tša Baithuti (NSFAS) se dirišana le  Sekhwama sa Inšorentshe ya bao ba Feletšwego ke Mešomo (UIF) go rarolla bothata bjo.
Dihlongwa tše tše pedi di sa tšwa go tsebagatša semmušo lenaneo la baithuti bao ba phethilego dithuto tša bona go fa baithuti ba 50 bao ba phethilego dithuto maitemogelo a mošomo ka makaleng a mehutahuta mo dikgweding tše 12.
Bakgopedi ba mešomo bao ba atlegilego ba tsentšwe ka dikantorong tša NSFAS ka Kapa Bodikela. Molaodi wa NSFAS, Ngaka Randall Carolissen, o boletše gore maikemišetšo a lesolo le ke go rarolla mathata a tlhokego ya mešomo ao bafsa ba naga ye ba lebanego le ona, kudukudu baholegi ba setlamo se sa thušo ya ditšhelete.
“Bjalo ka NSFAS, re ketapele go rarolleng ga dikgakanego tša baithuti ebile re ba mathomo ba go bona seabe seo tlhokego ya mešomo e nago le sona go baithuti ba bantši bao ba phethilego dithuto,” a realo.  Yo mongwe wa baholegi ba 50 ke Luchulumanco Nanto (24), yo a phethilego National Diploma ya Accounting ka Cape Peninsula Universi ty of Technology (CPUT) ka 2018.
O bolela gore o tsebile ka ga lenaneo le ka sehlopha sa kgašo ya setšhaba seo a welago go sona ka khamphaseng, moo yena le baithuti ka yena bao ba phethilego dithuto ba abelanago dibaka tša mešomo.
“Ke tlo hwetša tsebo ya go dira mošomo ka ga seo ke ithutetšego sona  gomme se gape se tla ntokišetša dinnete tša seemo sa mošomong,” gwa bolela Nanto, yo mo lebakeng le a ithutelago B-Tech ya Taxation  ka CPUT.
Nanto le bana ba gabo ba bane ba godišitšwe ke mma o tee yo a bego a palelwa ke go lefa ditšhelete tša yunibesithi.
“Tate o hlokofetše ge ke be ke sa na le mengwaga ye 10 fela,” gwa bolela Nanto, yo a tšwago King William’s Town ka Kapa Bohlabela.
Ka mahlatse, o hweditše pasari go tšwa go Setheo sa Theknolotši ya Tshedimošo ya Mmušo (SITA). Nanto o hlohleletša bafsa go nagana go tsenela lenaneo le la baithuti bao ba phethilego dithuto pele ga ge ba dira dikgopelo tša mešomo ya sa ruri ka ge le tla ba maatlafatša go bokgoni bja bona le go maitshwaro a mošomong ka lebelo la maleba mola ka go le lengwe ba ipshina ka go ba ka lefaseng la mošomo.

 THUTOEastern Cape school better equipped to teachSilusapho NyandaPupils of a rural school in the Eastern Cape rejoiced when their school was given a facelift and received computers.
The 213 pupils of Albertina Sisulu Senior Primary School in Xolobe village in Tsomo, will benefit from the donation of 30 computers, refurbishments and safer classes, thanks to the Industrial Development Corporation (IDC) and the Eastern Cape Department of Education.
“The pupils had to share the old computers we had but now they will be able to work on their own and easily become computer literate,” said the school’s principal, Nomfuneko Somlotha.She said the pupils will be better able to access informa tion for their school work and for research, whereas before they relied only on textbooks.
“Visual learning is important because it is easier to remem ber something that you have seen,” Somlotha told Vuk’uzen zele.
She said the equipment would also help speed up teaching because pupils could be referred to websites as an othe mode of learning.
The school was also given added security and furniture upgrades.
Somlotha said the school will use the donated equipment  to develop pupils’ knowledge beyond the classroom and to open their eyes to the many ca reer paths available to them.
